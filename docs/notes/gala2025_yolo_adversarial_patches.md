## Problem

- What threat model is assumed? White-box (gradients of Ultralytics YOLO models used); patches are naturalistic (GAN-generated); both digital evaluation and edge device deployment considered.
- What detector or classifier is attacked? Ultralytics YOLOv5, YOLOv8, YOLOv9, YOLOv10.
- What is the attack goal? Evaluate vulnerability of modern Ultralytics YOLO detectors to naturalistic adversarial patch attacks (realistic-looking patches that fool detectors). Specifically examines the security/efficiency tradeoff for Edge AI: smaller, faster models may be more vulnerable.

## Method

- Patch type: Naturalistic adversarial patches generated by optimizing the latent space of a pretrained GAN. Patches appear visually realistic (natural textures) while suppressing YOLO detections.
- Optimization method: GAN latent space optimization — adapts Hu et al. (2021)'s method to modern Ultralytics YOLO architectures.
- Loss terms: Not explicitly detailed in publicly available abstract; follows Hu et al. approach (minimize objectness for target class while latent priors enforce realism).
- Transformations / EoT details: Digital transforms during training (scale, rotation, blur, etc.) per standard practice.
- Physical-world considerations: Experiments on edge devices (smaller model deployment); physical testing implied but focus is on digital evaluation.

## Experimental Setup

- Dataset: INRIA Person dataset, MPII Human Pose dataset
- Target classes: Person (primary); vehicle classes mentioned
- Model versions: YOLOv5, YOLOv8, YOLOv9, YOLOv10 (all Ultralytics)
- Metrics: Detection evasion rate (drop in AP), fooling rate

## Results

- Main quantitative result: High success rates in evading detection across all tested YOLO versions.
- Key finding: **Larger models are generally more robust than smaller counterparts** — highlights a critical security tradeoff for Edge AI devices that rely on smaller, faster but more vulnerable models.
- What worked best: GAN-optimized naturalistic patches transferred well across Ultralytics YOLO variants.
- What failed or stayed weak: Full per-model AP numbers not available without Springer access; see the GitHub repo README for figures.

## Relevance to My Capstone

- Direct relevance to YOLO11: High — YOLO11 is the next generation after v10; the framework should transfer directly.
- Direct relevance to YOLO26: This is the research gap — Gala et al. do not cover YOLO26. Testing YOLO26 is a novel contribution my capstone can make.
