## Category Definitions

- **foundational**: Establishes core threat model, patch concept, or EoT framework
- **YOLO-specific**: Attacks or defenses designed specifically for YOLO detectors
- **transfer**: Studies transferability of adversarial patches across models or architectures
- **physical-world**: Empirical evaluation or design for physical deployment
- **naturalistic**: Patches designed to appear visually natural or avoid detection by humans
- **dynamic**: Patches designed for dynamic scenes or moving targets
- **defense**: Methods to detect, neutralize, or recover from adversarial patches
- **benchmark**: Systematic evaluation frameworks or comparative studies
- **survey**: Literature reviews or taxonomies
- **off-topic**: Not relevant to YOLO adversarial patches

## YOLO Relevance Definitions

- **direct**: Paper explicitly attacks or defends YOLO (any version)
- **partial**: Paper includes YOLO among multiple detectors, or relevant to YOLO by design
- **indirect**: Paper's findings are applicable to YOLO but YOLO is not evaluated
- **none**: Paper does not involve YOLO in any meaningful way
